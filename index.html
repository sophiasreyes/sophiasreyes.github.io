FAQs


Is homosexuality a choice?
No. Homosexuality is not a choice mainly because it is not the result of a decision or an ongoing daily effort. Just as how it is with straight people, who do not choose to be who they are. Many people discover their sexual orientation rather than choosing it during the early years of adolescence or before any romantic or sexual experience.

What is Sexual Orientation?
Sexual Orientation determines who you are sexually attracted to. However, it is more than just physical attraction towards another person, but rather, it is also about intimate feelings that are shared between two people. Sexual Orientation can be also explained by who meets those needs for you within a romantic relationship.

What is Gender Identity?
This describes what type of gender you feel inside, without considering the genitalia you are born with.

Can homosexuality be cured?
No, because initially, it is not a disorder therefore, there is no “cure”. The therapy which aims to change one’s sexual orientation is called reparative, conversion, or aversion therapy which is, according to the American Psychiatric Association, ineffective and psychologically harmful.

What does “faggot” and “dyke” mean and why is it considered offensive?
The term faggot is an insult towards a gay man. On the other hand, the term dyke is an insult towards a lesbian woman. This is offensive because by saying these words, people intend to disrespect gay and lesbian women and coming off as homophobic or someone who is not fond of homosexuality.
